Cloth Perception Experiment

This experiment is meant to study human perception of the stiffness of various cloths. For each trial, you will be shown triplets of short cloth videos (5-6 seconds). The middle video is the base video, or the anchor video -- the video to the left displays Cloth A, while the video to the right displays Cloth B. You have to decide if Cloth A or Cloth B has a different stiffness than the anchor.

There are 10 blocks in the whole experiment. For each block, there are 50 triplets for a total of 500.

After filling in response for each video, press the next arrow or press the Enter key to move to next video.

Please make sure not to skip any video and look at the motion of cloths before making any decision; don't just go by their visual appearance.

The entire experiment takes around 1 hour.

When all triplets of videos have been labeled, you will be moved to the the next block.

Please write your intials in the same way for each block.
