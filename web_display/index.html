Cloth Perception Experiment


Please read all of the instructions, they are all important.


Instructions:


This experiment is studying human perception of cloth stiffness. In this experiment you will be shown triplets of small length videos one by one. The videos are 5-6 seconds in length. In each trial, you are shown 3 videos. The middle video is the anchor video. The left video is cloth A, the right video is cloth B.


You have to decide whether cloth A or cloth B has a different stiffness than the anchor. In other words, find the odd one out in stiffness. You will also be asked to rate the triplet from 0 to 10 based on how confident you were in your decision. 0 means you are completely confident that you were correct, while 10 means your choice was a complete guess. Use the Next button to see the next triplet of videos.


At the start of each block, you will be prompted to enter your initials. Please enter them the same way evey time. At the end of each block, a new tab will open automatically and prompt you for your initials. When the experiment is over, a new tab will open telling you experiment is finished.


You can take breaks between blocks (when a new tab opens and you are prompted for your initials, before the videos are displayed). However, we ask that you do not take a break in the middle of a block. There are 10 blocks in the entire experiment. There are 50 trials for each block. The entire experiment takes around 60 minutes.



When you are ready, you can get started by pressing the button below.
